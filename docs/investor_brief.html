The Thesis

Physical climate risk is the largest unpriced externality in European financial markets. The satellite data to measure it exists — and is free. What has not existed is a platform that fuses it at cell-level precision and delivers it simultaneously to parametric insurers, regulatory compliance teams, and government emergency managers. We built that platform.

Hard Questions

Objections — and why they're wrong.

These are the five questions every serious investor or reinsurance cat desk will ask. Answered directly, not deflected.

Objection 01 — Most common

"Copernicus is free. Can't anyone just build this in six months?"

Raised by: Seed VCs · Technical due diligence

Yes — the data is free. The 14-month SAR processing pipeline, multi-source fusion at three different spatial resolutions, and XGBoost models calibrated against three specific historical European events are not. A new entrant starts without that history.

More importantly: free inputs are our cost structure advantage, not a vulnerability. Any competitor choosing commercial satellite data (Planet Labs, Maxar) carries a real OpEx line we don't have. We can price 30% below them and still run 70–80% gross margins. They cannot fight that war.

The Bloomberg framing: Bloomberg doesn't own Fed policy announcements. They charge for the terminal that turns public data into a financial instrument. We don't own Copernicus. We charge for the certified trigger index and regulatory package built on it.

SAR processing pipeline: 6mo to build BaFin/Lloyd's cert: 18–24mo minimum Model calibration: 3 historical events
Objection 02

"Descartes Underwriting raised $141M and has Munich Re. Why not just partner with them?"

Raised by: Strategic investors · Reinsurers

Descartes operates as an MGA — they take underwriting risk on the book. We are a data platform — we take no risk. These are different business models aimed at different buyers. Descartes sells to the cedant side (corporates buying coverage). We sell to the capacity side (cat desks designing products) and the compliance side (CFOs filing CSRD).

Descartes also works at 5–10km resolution and has no regulatory compliance product. A reinsurer who uses Descartes for one product still needs a separate solution for CSRD and a separate data provider for operations. We consolidate all three onto one certified score.

Their $141M valuation validates the category. Our addressable surface (parametric + regulatory + govt) is wider, at 14× finer resolution, with 70%+ gross margins vs. their MGA economics.

Objection 03

"Your models are trained on three events. That's not statistically robust."

Raised by: Technical investors · Reinsurance actuaries

Fair challenge — and exactly why the design fee exists. We don't claim the model is universally pre-calibrated. The trigger design phase validates basis risk against the specific client's actual loss history for their specific geography. Generic model + client-specific calibration is the correct architecture for parametric, not a single global model.

ERA5 also provides 40 years of reanalysis data. We can retrospectively score any European location at any point since 1984, which means a client with a 10-year claims history can validate the index against all 10 years before the first live policy is written.

ERA5 reanalysis covers 1984–present. Every prospective client's historical exposure can be back-tested against our index before contract.

Objection 04

"CSRD compliance tooling is commoditising fast. Why won't this be free in two years?"

Raised by: Enterprise SaaS investors · Banks

Generic CSRD disclosure tooling (carbon calculators, ESG reporting platforms) is commoditising. Physical risk scoring at 0.74km² cell level is not — it requires satellite data fusion that no SaaS startup is going to give away. The commodity is the reporting wrapper; the defensible part is the hyper-local risk score beneath it.

More importantly: our CSRD product shares the same score engine as our parametric product. A bank that buys compliance is already holding the data infrastructure that lets them offer parametric coverage to corporate clients. That cross-sell is not available from any pure-play CSRD tool.

Objection 05

"Why would a reinsurer trust a startup index over RMS or AIR?"

Raised by: Reinsurance cat desks · Lloyd's syndicates

RMS and AIR produce cat models for retrospective loss estimation and capital allocation — they are not parametric trigger indices. No current RMS or AIR product produces a daily H3-cell score that can serve as a parametric payout trigger. The market gap is real.

The trust question is answered by certification (BaFin, Lloyd's, AMF) and by the back-testing process in the design fee engagement. A cat desk that has back-tested our index against their own loss history across ten years of ERA5 data, reviewed the SHAP factor decomposition, and seen the basis risk analysis is not trusting a startup. They are trusting a certified, auditable index.

Parametric insurance as a category was built by startups, not by RMS. Arbol, Descartes, Kettle — none of these started with a 30-year brand. They started with a better index design. So did we.
